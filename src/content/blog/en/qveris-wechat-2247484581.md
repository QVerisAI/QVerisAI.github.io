Have you noticed that in financial markets, the people who make money are often not the smartest ones, but the ones with the fastest information?

To check one data point, you have to open five websites. To compare two stocks, you manually copy and paste. When you see a piece of news, you do not know whether it is true.

Where is the problem?

Information is a weapon, but the blade in your hand is dull.



Last Wednesday, a friend posted a screenshot in a group chat. A stock had suddenly surged 8%, and he was asking, "Can I chase it?"

I opened my market data app to check the candlestick chart, then opened another website to look at capital flows, then a third one to check the Dragon and Tiger List. By the time I pieced those three signals together, three minutes had passed. The stock was still rising, but I no longer dared to enter, because I did not know what was behind that 8% move: institutions accumulating, or retail traders rushing in.

**Those three minutes are the cost of the information gap.**

Many platforms will tell you: we have 100,000+ data indicators.

What they do not say is that those 100,000 indicators are scattered across eight different pages. You have to find them yourself, stitch them together yourself, and judge them yourself. By the time you finish, the opportunity is already gone.

We are different.

Our data has a delay of a few seconds. It is not the fastest, but it is enough for most traders. The interface is not flashy, but the indicators you need can be found in three seconds.

We are not Bloomberg, but we want every retail investor to be able to afford professional data.

How do we do it?

Deep A-share data from Hundsun Juyuan, global market data from EODHD, and U.S. equity financials from Financial Modeling Prep: data sources that used to require separate contracts and separate integrations are now all connected into one capability network.

You no longer chase after data. You ask, and the answer comes to you.
