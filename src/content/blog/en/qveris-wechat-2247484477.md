A friend asked me: "Moutai has fallen from 2,600 to 1,400, nearly 50%. Is it time to buy the dip?"

I did not answer directly. Instead, I asked: "Do you know what Moutai's valuation is now? How fast are its earnings growing? Are institutions buying or selling?"

He could not answer. This is not unusual. Most retail investors watch stock prices rise and fall, but do not look at the numbers underneath.

Today, using tested data, we analyze Moutai's current investment value across four dimensions: valuation, earnings, capital flows, and expectations.

**Conclusion: based on P/E, Moutai has gone from expensive to reasonable, but it is not yet cheap.**

Historical comparison: when the stock price was RMB 2,600 in 2021, its P/E exceeded 60x. Today, at around RMB 1,400, the P/E is 21x. The valuation has compressed by 65%. That is the main reason for the stock price decline: valuation contraction, not earnings collapse.

**But what does a 21x P/E mean in the baijiu industry? Here is the comparison**:

- Wuliangye P/E: about 18x

- Luzhou Laojiao P/E: about 16x

- Shanxi Fenjiu P/E: about 20x

Moutai's valuation premium has narrowed from about 2x in the past to roughly 1.2x. Whether that premium is justified depends on how much you think Moutai's brand moat is worth.

**What retail investors should focus on: can you buy at 21x P/E?** That depends on your expectations for growth. If Moutai can maintain 15% growth, 21x P/E is not expensive. If growth falls below 10%, there is still room for the P/E to decline.

II. Earnings Analysis

## Earnings Analysis: Growth Has Slowed from 20% to 10%, and Expectations Have Already Been Lowered

![](../../../assets/blog-qveris-wechat-2247484477-3.jpg)

Valuation is the price in the numerator; earnings are the profit in the denominator. Before looking at valuation, look at earnings first.

**Moutai's 2025 annual report data (already disclosed)**:

- Total operating revenue: about RMB 150 billion, up about 11% year on year

- Net profit: about RMB 75 billion, up about 13% year on year

- Gross margin: 91.9%, still formidable

- Net margin: 52%, the ceiling of the baijiu industry

**The earnings are fine, but the growth slowdown is real.** Growth has fallen from above 20% in the past to the 10-15% range. This is a challenge facing the overall baijiu industry: penetration of high-end baijiu is approaching its ceiling, and the shift toward younger consumers remains slow.

**But there is a cognitive trap here.** Many retail investors see that Moutai's earnings are still growing and assume the stock price should rise. But stock prices reflect expectations, not the past. The market is not worried about 2025 earnings. It is worried about whether Moutai can sustain growth in 2026 and 2027.

Institutional research reports generally expect Moutai's growth over the next three years to be in the 10-12% range. If that proves true, the current 21x P/E is broadly reasonable, but it will be difficult for it to drive a sharp rise in the stock price.

**What retail investors should focus on: if you buy now, what money are you trying to make?**

- If you are trying to earn from earnings growth: 10-12% annualized returns require long-term holding

- If you are trying to earn from valuation recovery: if the P/E returns from 21x to 30x, there is 40% upside, but it requires support from market sentiment

- If you are trying to earn from dividends: a 3.66% dividend yield is better than bank wealth management products, but stock price volatility risk is high

III. Capital Flows

## Capital Flows: Institutional Selling Does Not Mean the Company Has Deteriorated, but Buying Power Has Clearly Weakened

![](../../../assets/blog-qveris-wechat-2247484477-4.jpg)

Using data tools to check changes in Moutai's institutional holdings, we found several signals worth noting.

**Northbound funds (Hong Kong Securities Clearing)**: holdings of 55 million shares, accounting for 4.4%. Since 2024, the overall trend has been outflows, showing weaker foreign investor interest in the baijiu sector.

**Public funds**: holdings account for about 6.87%, but the structure is highly divergent. Passive index funds, such as the SSE 50 ETF and CSI 300 ETF, are the main holders, while actual holdings by actively managed funds are declining.

**Focus on the comparison between two data sets:**

At the end of 2024, the top five active funds with heavy Moutai positions were: E Fund Blue Chip Selected (2.23 million shares, Zhang Kun), Invesco Great Wall Emerging Growth (1.13 million shares, Liu Yanchun), China Universal Consumer Industry (420,000 shares), Fullgoal Tianhui Selected Growth (800,000 shares, Zhu Shaoxing), and China Merchants CSI Baijiu Index (4.82 million shares, passive index).

**Signal interpretation**: star fund managers such as Zhang Kun and Liu Yanchun are still holding Moutai, but their positions have been cut in half compared with the 2021 peak. They are not clearing out. They are reducing exposure.

**New capital in Q1 2025**: mainly passive allocation from CSI A500 index funds, not active buying.

**What retail investors should focus on: is institutional selling a bad thing?**

Not necessarily. Institutional reductions do not mean the company has deteriorated. They may simply be reallocating to other sectors with better growth prospects, such as AI or new energy. But for the stock price, when buying power weakens, it becomes harder for the stock to rise.

Moutai's shareholder structure has one distinctive feature: Moutai Group plus state-owned capital together hold more than 60%, and this portion is basically stable. The truly tradable float is less than 40%, which amplifies the impact of fund position changes on the stock price.

IV. Future Catalyst

## Future Catalyst: Price Increase Potential and the Younger-Consumer Challenge Coexist

![](../../../assets/blog-qveris-wechat-2247484477-5.jpg)

![](../../../assets/blog-qveris-wechat-2247484477-6.jpg)

Investing is about the future. What catalysts does Moutai have ahead?

**Positive factors include price increase expectations** (the official guide price of Feitian Moutai is RMB 1,499, while the market price is above RMB 2,000, so there is theoretically still room for price increases), **channel reform** (higher direct sales share and optimized profit structure), **higher dividends** (ample cash flow and room for a higher payout ratio, currently about 50%), and **valuation recovery** (if market risk appetite improves, the P/E could return to the 25-30x range).

**Negative factors include the younger-consumer challenge** (acceptance of baijiu is declining among those born in the 1990s and 2000s), **inventory pressure** (dealer inventory levels are relatively high, leaving less room for channel stuffing), **slower growth** (from 20% down to 10%, pushing the valuation center lower), and **capital outflows** (continued northbound fund reductions and lower institutional allocation).

**What retail investors should focus on: if you buy now, how long do you need to wait?**

Short term (within 1 year): catalysts are not obvious, and the stock price is likely to fluctuate

Medium term (1-3 years): earnings growth plus valuation recovery may deliver annualized returns of 10-15%

Long term (more than 3 years): depends on whether Moutai can solve the younger-consumer challenge, and uncertainty increases

Back to the original question: can you buy the dip in Moutai at RMB 1,400?

**My view**: after Moutai fell from 2,600 to 1,400, most of the valuation risk has already been released. A 21x P/E and 3.66% dividend yield are attractive for long-term investors. But do not expect a quick rebound. Institutional capital is still flowing out, and market sentiment needs time to recover.

**Execution strategy**:

- If your position is light: you can build gradually, adding once for every 5% decline

- If your position is heavy: wait for fundamental catalysts, such as a price increase or better-than-expected earnings, before adding

- If you are trading short term: participation is not recommended, because volatility is high and the catalyst is unclear
