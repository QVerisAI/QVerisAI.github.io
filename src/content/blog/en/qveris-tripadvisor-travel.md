In many travel planning, recommendation, and place-review scenarios, real and trustworthy data is essential. We recently connected QVeris to the Tripadvisor Content API, giving agents a powerful new real-world capability.

Tripadvisor is one of the world’s leading travel review and recommendation platforms, with more than 8 million places, including hotels, restaurants, and attractions; 1 billion reviews and ratings from real travelers; and multilingual content.

![qveris-tripadvisor-travel-1](../../../assets/blog-qveris-tripadvisor-travel-1.jpg)

In the past, when an agent needed to generate place recommendations, compare ratings, or summarize reviews, it often had to rely on training-time knowledge, scraped data, or rough estimates. That could easily lead to inaccurate or unreliable results.

Now, by accessing this API through QVeris, agents can:

- 📍 **Retrieve specific place information**: including names, addresses, ratings, and more for hotels, restaurants, and attractions
- ⭐ **Search real user reviews and ratings**: so recommendations are backed by data
- 📸 **Use traveler photos**: making travel suggestions more credible
- 🔍 **Run location search and filtering**: such as finding places by rating, type, distance, and other criteria

This means agents no longer just “sound right” or “make a likely guess.” They can truly make judgments and recommendations based on real-world data.

For example, users can ask an agent:

- “Help me find a list of hotels in Tokyo rated 4.5★ or higher with recent positive reviews”
- “Rank the highest-rated restaurants in Paris by score”
- “List highly rated attractions near a city, along with review summaries”

![qveris-tripadvisor-travel-2](../../../assets/blog-qveris-tripadvisor-travel-2.jpg)

As you can see, by calling Tripadvisor data, the agent can independently produce a detailed and accurate hotel list.

Behind these tasks, the model is not “guessing the answer.” Instead, the agent **actively calls the Tripadvisor API, understands the meaning of the data, and combines the results into a useful output**.

QVeris is making this API available for free for everyone to use. Come try it out! 👇
