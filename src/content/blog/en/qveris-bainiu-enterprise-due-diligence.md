## When Investigating a Company, the Hard Part Is Not Finding Its Name

Most of the time, users are not really asking, "What is this company called?"

The real questions are usually: Who is this company, exactly? Who controls it behind the scenes? Does it carry judicial, administrative, or credit-default risks? Is it still genuinely operating? Has it recently been hiring, financing, bidding, or expanding? Do its trademarks, patents, and external investments show that it has real business capabilities?

In the past, these questions were difficult to answer with a single search. Corporate registration data lived in one place, equity and executive information in another, while judicial risks, administrative penalties, tenders, recruitment, financing, and intellectual property were scattered across still more data sources. In manual due diligence, the typical workflow was to open multiple websites, copy the company name, search repeatedly, take screenshots, assemble tables, and finally rely on human judgment to connect the clues.

With the new Bainiu Provider integration, QVeris aims to solve exactly this problem: enabling Agents not merely to "find a company," but to organize fragmented public enterprise data into a readable, follow-up-ready, reusable analysis workflow, much like an enterprise due diligence radar.

>
> Enterprise due diligence is not about checking a single field. It is about connecting identity, relationships, risk, operations, and commercial signals. With Bainiu integrated, QVeris Agents can keep asking questions along that chain.
>

## What QVeris Agents Can See After the Bainiu Integration

Bainiu's core value is bringing multidimensional public enterprise information into the workflow.

It does not provide only a business registration lookup. It covers multiple dimensions, including company profiles, equity relationships, risk and compliance, operating activity, commercial opportunities, and intellectual property. For QVeris Agents, this means an enterprise analysis can continue beyond "basic information" instead of stopping at company name, legal representative, and registered capital.

## Layer One: Company Profile, First Confirm Who It Is

The first step in any due diligence process is not to rush to a conclusion, but to confirm the subject.

Companies with the same name, historical names, group companies, branches, and affiliated companies can easily cause errors in manual searches. Bainiu's enterprise super search and in-depth company information capabilities help Agents first align the target company's basic identity: company name, Unified Social Credit Code, legal representative, registered capital, establishment date, operating status, registered address, official website, industry classification, geographic location, and more.

![](../../../assets/blog-qveris-bainiu-enterprise-due-diligence-2.png)

This step may look basic, but it is critical.

If an Agent has not confirmed whether it is looking at the right company, every subsequent analysis of risk, equity, financing, tenders, and bids may go off track. After integrating Bainiu, QVeris can first establish a company profile, then move into the next layer of analysis.

## Layer Two: Equity and Relationships, See Who Is Connected Behind It

Enterprise due diligence often becomes truly interesting when relationships enter the picture.

A company may appear to be an independent entity, but who are its shareholders? Who are its executives? Have there been important shareholder changes in its history? Which companies has it invested in? Where else does its legal representative hold positions? Do these relationships reveal a group structure, capital network, supply chain, or business network behind it?

Bainiu's capabilities for shareholders and executives, historical shareholders and executives, external corporate investments, and the legal representative's external investments and positions allow QVeris Agents to expand a company from "a point" into "a relationship map."

This is useful in many scenarios. Investment teams can examine the capital and control relationships behind a company. Sales teams can identify group customers and affiliated companies. Risk control teams can check whether legal representatives, executives, and external investments show anomalies. BD teams can find potential collaboration paths through enterprise relationships.

![](../../../assets/blog-qveris-bainiu-enterprise-due-diligence-3.png)

![](../../../assets/blog-qveris-bainiu-enterprise-due-diligence-4.png)

The advantage of an Agent is that it does more than list fields. It can explain relationships: who the key controllers are, which external investments deserve attention, and whether historical shareholder changes suggest shifts in business stage.

## Layer Three: Risk and Compliance, Look Beyond the Highlights to the Hidden Signals

When evaluating a company, it is not enough to look at its website and financing news.

Real due diligence must examine risk: Are there records of dishonest judgment debtors? Is there judicial assistance? Are there administrative penalties? Are there administrative licenses? Are there penalties related to tax or financial regulation? This information often does not appear in a company's own introduction, but it is essential for assessing cooperation risk, performance capability, and compliance status.

After integrating Bainiu, QVeris Agents can build a set of risk and compliance checks around judicial risk, administrative penalties, administrative licenses, credit-default information, regulatory penalties, and related items. Users do not need to manually browse page after page. The Agent can first produce a structured summary, then highlight high-risk clues that require human review.

This capability is especially suitable for basic due diligence before supplier admission, risk checks before signing with a partner, initial screening of investment targets, background reviews of customers or channel partners, and high-compliance scenarios such as finance, government and enterprise services, and tendering.

![](../../../assets/blog-qveris-bainiu-enterprise-due-diligence-5.png)

>
> The absence of negative records is itself a signal. At the same time, a large number of administrative license records can indicate ongoing operational traces in areas such as production, construction, safety, and environmental compliance.
>

The point here is not for the Agent to replace legal judgment. It is to help surface public risk signals first and reduce the chance of missing them.

## Layer Four: Operating Activity, Read Real Operations Through Hiring, Contact Information, and Addresses

Whether a company is "truly active" cannot be judged only by its registration status.

Some companies remain normal in business registration records, but their websites have not been updated for a long time, hiring has stopped, contact information is inconsistent, and actual office locations are unclear. Others may be small, yet continue hiring, maintain stable public contact information, and have clear office locations, indicating that they are still expanding or operating.

Bainiu provides data such as recruitment information, commonly used contact details, official websites, geographic locations, and actual office addresses, helping QVeris Agents assess company status from the operating side.

For example, job openings can reflect business direction: more technical roles may indicate R&D investment; more sales and channel roles may suggest market expansion; a large number of regional roles may indicate offline business rollout. Contact information and addresses can also help assess a company's reachability and real operating location.

This moves Agent-based company analysis beyond "how much registered capital does it have" and closer to everyday business judgment: Is it still moving? In what direction? Are its actions consistent with the business it claims to run?

## Layer Five: Commercial Opportunities, Find Business Signals Through Financing and Tenders

Enterprise data is not only for risk prevention. It can also be used to find opportunities.

Financing events can indicate a company's stage and the level of capital-market attention it receives. Tender and bidding information can reveal which projects the company has participated in, which customers it has served, and where it has cooperation or competition relationships. External investments and intellectual property can also help determine whether the company is expanding into new businesses or has accumulated long-term assets.

After integrating Bainiu, QVeris Agents can organize these signals into business-oriented questions: Has this company recently raised financing? Does it frequently participate in tenders and bids? Who are its partners and competitors? Does it have accumulated assets such as trademarks and patents? Does it look more like a real operating entity or an empty shell?

>
> Operating activity and commercial opportunities are often assessed together. Recruitment, contact information, financing, tenders, and trademark information can jointly indicate whether a company is still continuously operating.
>

![](../../../assets/blog-qveris-bainiu-enterprise-due-diligence-6.png)

This is valuable for sales, investment research, BD, procurement, and industry analysis. The Agent can first organize the company's commercial activity signals, then help users decide whether to keep following up, conduct focused review, or lower priority.
